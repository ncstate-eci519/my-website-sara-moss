About Mrs. Moss

The Basics

I currently live in Carrboro, NC. In December, I started a new job as a virtual teacher to an awesome group of kindergartners and first graders at an online charter school in my home-state of Michigan called Uplift Michigan Online School. Although I enjoyed teaching in traditional brick-and-mortar schools for several years, I love teaching online.

My Favorite Educational Tech Tools

  • Freckle Education

    I discovered Freckle Education when one of my favorite tools, TenMarks was retired. Freckle makes it easier for teachers to provide students with individualized learning. I love how easy it is to assign the same article content to all my students automatically based on their reading level. I also love that they are able to work at their own level by going through the system's adaptive side.

  • Google Suite

    I contemplated whether I put Google on as a favorite tech tool since it is so integrated into many people's lives already. However, since Google is part of my daily teaching life and my job comes a hault anytime their services crash, I think it deserved a place on the list. I love the ways the Google Suite seemlessly integrates with my school's LMS, Buzz. Additionally, the tools available on the Google Suite make it a one-stop-shop for most of the things teachers and students need.

  • Twine

    I haven't used Twine with students yet because I teach littles. However, it seems like one of the coolest things! On Twine, you can use basic coding skills to practice writing. Students can easily create make-your-own-adventure stories as well as use the program to draft a paper and really see the various iterations.

  • EdPuzzle

    I used EdPuzzle every week with my 3rd-5th graders. EdPuzzle is a tool that allows educators to insert voiceover as well as formative assessment questions throughout the video. Once assigned to students, they will go to EdPuzzle and watch the video. EdPuzzle tracks their viewing as well as their answers to the questions. Students are not able to go to another tab or it will stop the video from playing. Students are able to rewatch the video in order to get the questions correct. Teachers can upload their own videos or they can choose videos from YouTube, Khan Academy, and more directly from the EdPuzzle video library.
